<p><p>Nonhormonal Intrauterine Contraceptive Devices are small, T-shaped devices inserted into the uterus to prevent pregnancy. They work by creating a hostile environment for sperm, preventing fertilization. Examples include copper IUDs. On the other hand, Hormonal Intrauterine Contraceptive Devices release progestin, a synthetic hormone that thickens cervical mucus and thins the uterine lining to prevent pregnancy. Examples include hormonal IUDs. Both types offer long-term and highly effective contraception options for women.</p></p>
