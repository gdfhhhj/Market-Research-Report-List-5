<p><p>Grid-connected microgrids come in three main types: AC microgrids, DC microgrids, and hybrid microgrids. AC microgrids utilize alternating current to connect to the main grid, allowing for easier integration of renewable energy sources. DC microgrids, on the other hand, use direct current and are more efficient for certain applications like data centers. Hybrid microgrids combine both AC and DC technologies to maximize benefits and flexibility, making them a popular choice for communities seeking reliable and sustainable power solutions.</p></p>
