<p><p>Manual steering racks are commonly classified into two main types - rack and pinion type, and ball screw type. </p><p>Rack and pinion type steering racks use a pinion gear attached to the steering wheel that rotates a horizontal rack, allowing for easy and precise steering control. On the other hand, ball screw type steering racks use a threaded shaft with ball bearings to convert rotational motion into linear motion, providing smoother and more accurate steering response. Both types are commonly used in manual steering systems in automobiles.</p></p>
