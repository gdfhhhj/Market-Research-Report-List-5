<p><p>Surfactants in the agrochemical market are classified into four types based on their ionic charge: nonionic, anionic, cationic, and amphoteric. Nonionic surfactants are versatile and compatible with various pesticides. Anionic surfactants have a negative charge and are commonly used in herbicides. Cationic surfactants have a positive charge and are effective in improving the spreading and wetting properties of fungicides. Amphoteric surfactants can be both positively and negatively charged, providing a balanced performance in a wide range of agrochemical applications.</p></p>
