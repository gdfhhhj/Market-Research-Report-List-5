Air-cooled micro fuel cells use air as the cooling agent to dissipate heat generated during the power generation process, while liquid-cooled micro fuel cells use a liquid coolant, such as water, to regulate the temperature of the fuel cell. Both types have their own advantages and applications in various industries.</p></p>
