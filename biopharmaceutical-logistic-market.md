<p><p>Biopharmaceutical logistics can be categorized into two main markets: cold chain logistics and non-cold chain logistics. Cold chain logistics involves the transportation and storage of temperature-sensitive biopharmaceutical products, such as vaccines and biologics, in controlled environments to maintain their efficacy. On the other hand, non-cold chain logistics deals with biopharmaceutical products that do not require strict temperature controls. Both markets play a crucial role in ensuring the integrity and quality of biopharmaceutical products throughout the supply chain.</p></p>
