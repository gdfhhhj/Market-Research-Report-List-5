<p><p>Enhanced Oil Recovery (EOR) is a technique used to extract more crude oil from reservoirs by utilizing various methods such as CO2 Enhanced Oil Recovery, Nitrogen Enhanced Oil Recovery, Polymer Flooding, Surfactant Flooding, Alkaline Flooding, Micellar Flooding, and others. CO2 EOR involves injecting carbon dioxide into the reservoir to increase oil recovery. Nitrogen EOR involves injecting nitrogen gas to displace oil. Polymer, surfactant, and alkaline flooding involve injecting chemicals to improve oil flow. Micellar flooding involves using surfactants mixed with water to recover oil efficiently. Other methods include thermal, microbial, and chemical flooding.</p></p>
