<p><p>Edge emitting semiconductor lasers are categorized into different types based on their design and functionality. Distributed Feedback Lasers feature a grating structure for wavelength selection. Distributed Bragg Reflector Lasers have a mirror at one end to enhance output. Fabry-Perot Lasers use parallel mirrors to generate a single longitudinal mode. Broad Area Laser Diodes have a wider emitting area for higher power output. Each type of laser has specific advantages and applications in fields such as telecommunications, medical devices, and industrial processes.</p></p>
